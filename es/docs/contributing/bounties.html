How It Worksâ€‹

1. Find a Bountyâ€‹

Check available bounties:

  • GitHub Issues with "bounty" label
  • Discord #bounties channel
  • This page (featured bounties below)

2. Claim a Bountyâ€‹

Comment on the issue to claim:

I'd like to work on this bounty. 

Approach: [Brief description of your plan]
Timeline: [Estimated completion]

Wait for confirmation before starting.

3. Complete the Workâ€‹

  • Follow the bounty requirements
  • Adhere to Content Guidelines
  • Submit a pull request
  • Reference the bounty issue

4. Review & Approvalâ€‹

  • Maintainers review your submission
  • May request changes
  • Once approved, bounty is confirmed

5. Receive Paymentâ€‹

After merge:

  • Provide your NAM address (transparent)
  • Payment sent within 7 days
  • Transaction confirmed in public log

Bounty Guidelinesâ€‹

Quality Requirementsâ€‹

  • Must meet Content Guidelines
  • Must be original work
  • Must be accurate and verified
  • Must be properly formatted

Claim Rulesâ€‹

  • One bounty per person at a time (exceptions for quick fixes)
  • Claim expires after 14 days without progress
  • Communication required - update on status
  • Can be reassigned if inactive

Paymentâ€‹

  • Paid in NAM to your specified address
  • Amounts may vary based on quality
  • Exceptional work may receive bonus
  • Payments logged publicly for transparency

FAQâ€‹

How are bounty amounts determined?â€‹

Based on:

  • Effort required
  • Skill level needed
  • Value to community
  • Comparable market rates

Can I propose my own bounty?â€‹

Yes! Suggest bounties via GitHub issues. We evaluate based on community need.

What if my work isn't accepted?â€‹

We provide feedback and opportunity to revise. Significant work rarely goes unrewarded.

Can I work on multiple bounties?â€‹

Generally one at a time, but quick fixes can overlap with larger bounties.

How do I get paid?â€‹

Provide a NAM address (transparent) after work is approved. Payment within 7 days.

Are bounties taxable?â€‹

Consult a tax professional. You're responsible for reporting income in your jurisdiction.
